I

How I work

The room is the product

Staging, signal flow and timing are designed backwards from the feeling you want at minute 90 — not forwards from a checklist.

People before programs

A nervous keynote, a chairperson who needs talking points, a technician with ten seconds to react — my job is making each of them succeed.

Automate the second time

Anything done twice becomes a system. Human attention goes where machines can't: the room, the relationships, the judgement calls.

II

The AI layer

My background isn't the classic one. I haven't spent the last ten years calling myself an AI consultant or pretending to have all the answers. What I've done is wrestle with it in practice — tools, workflows, local models, agents — trying to understand what's actually useful, where the real friction is, and what separates genuine value from empty promises.

Some of that has been exciting. Some of it has felt a bit like voluntarily chewing glass. But when something finally clicks, you get a glimpse of what's possible, and it becomes very hard to look away. I run WhenIPO, a private-markets tracker I built and operate myself — and the site you're reading renders itself from a structured database of my own work.
